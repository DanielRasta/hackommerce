Creating a Merchant



By Eliad Saporta

After training multiple Payments Service Providers on how to best use the Coriunder system we decided it is important to write our methods.

The process always startes with a "Lead", using our MSU Javascript or our MSU mobile app, collect the info from potential customers either by placing the signup form on your site or social media channels - every submission will arrive at the "Registration" section which we will cover next.

Registration

Once a Lead was submitted a notification will be sent to the designated Admin User responsible for dealing with incoming Leads, we see it as a 4 steps process:
Step 1 - Review
The service allows you flexability of which data you which to collect, once a lead is submitted use the Registration section to review the Lead and take action by contacting the lead for further information.

Step 2 - Add
Use every contact with the lead to collect additional data and add it to the lead (Dont forget to SAVE it)

Step 3 - Approve
After you fill you have enough data about the lead to move to the next level start by approving the data and plan for the next steps.
We provide integrations to 3rd parties to forward the info to such as ZOHO, banks and more - if one of the available 3rd parties is not relevant move to the next step.

Step 4 - Create
Under the integration tab you will find the "Create a merchant" button- once clicked a new merchant will be created and the new merchant ID will populate in the lead list - click the ID to move to the newly create merchant profile.
From this moment on any update to the lead info on the Registration section will not effect the Merchant profile, use the Merchant management screens to keep managing the "lead" now turned "Merchant".
By default the newly created merchant's status is "NEW".


Merchant management

The merchant management "tabs" are important for your day to day operations, please pay attention while creating a merchant, any miscofiguration may result in processing issues and comunication issues.
The tutorials will include full details of each field on each tab - this will be an overview of the available tabs and tips to remember.

Summery
The summery tab serves as an overview of the merchant status, you can find quick links to the merchant's MCP and summery of transations and settlements.
You will be able to see a summery of fees collected, Rolling Reserve and merchant Balance, all by Currency and all relevant currencies.

Data
On the Data tab you should fill all of the information about the Company, Point of contact, and internal settings such as Account manage, Group, Parent company and more.
The most important part is to fill the Address of the merchant and the URL of the site that will be used - remember the system will run a duplicate check on all of the data to make sure no other merchant on the system is using them.

Process
The "Process" tab is responsible for the general settings of the processing sent to the system, from Recurring settings, silent post and more, as an Admin you set limits for certain features to minimize risk.

Risk
The Risk tab is responsible for all the settings relevant to check preformed on each transaction the merchant wishes to process, from AVS, limits, IP restrictions and more.
Learn about the merchant anticipated volume from the information the merchant submitted as part of the lead created in the system - tailor the Risk rules and limits according to his volume - Remember - any miscofiguration will result in limitations in processing.

Payout
When working in a "Payment Facilitator" modal you have responsiblity on the payout schedule and process - the system will calculate the "Holding margin" and will prepare the settlements according the configured date.

Terminals
We supports "Payment optimization" which means we direct every transaction to the relevant MID configured - to each merchant an "internal" terminal will be created per payment method per currency - you will be able to redirect transaction by territory and different currencies.

Fees
On the Fees tab you will configure all the fees to charge the merchants you onboard - from Monthly fees, setup fee to graded processing.
If you dont configure fees at this section you can always generate an "Admin" transaction to charge the fee you are looking to charge - bare in mind, the sell rate is configured under the Terminals tab and if misconfigured processing fees will not be deducted from the transactions.

Email
The Admin solution includes a "Mini CRM" solution, we call it "Mini" because we believe that we have a lot of things to improve on this feature and we know that there are a lot of providers that focus on CRM solutions for email tracking and more.
Our solution is focused on the merchant life cycle and payment processing - the Email tab of the Merchant management will include all emails filed under the merchant as part of the usage of the "Inbox" tool which monitors a selective email accounts as chosen by you.

Regulations
The "Regulations" tab includes a text editor connect to the Hosted Payment Page (HPP) provided as part of our solution, we have to make sure that the card holder review and accept the same terms and condition that the Acquiring bank approved as part of the merchant onboarding.
Once saved, the text will be loaded at every HPP load, make sure you notify the merchant to keep you updated at any time about any Terms and condition change and include a paragraph in your service agreement with the merchant.

Hosted
Our PCI Level 1 Hosted Payment Page is provided to all merchants who are not PCI compliant, from the Hosted tab we control the settings of the processing via the HPP, from required fields to the allowed payment methods.
Our "Product pages" are also part of the Hosted solution and the permissions are also controled from the Hosted tab.

Stored PM
We deal with a merchant's payment methods in two ways, collecting fees and paying out - the first merchant every client is setting up is their own merchant for the fee collection and with that merchant we charge the fees set forth in the fees tab.
When setting up the bank account pay attention to the account number not make a mistake when handling the wire transfers following the settlment.

Its all about following the flow - When it comes to merchant onboarding "templates" are not the way to go, each merchant deserves the attention of creating its profile tab by tab.

Emails

Our system sends out multiple emails regarding to the "life cycle" of your merchnats - this will be a short description of these emails and how you can modify them.

Merchant Registration
Once you review the initial lead you can choose to send the merchant a "Registration" email with general information about the service - Remember we provide generic email templates with your whitelabel, we recomend visiting the "Email templates" section under the settings menu and configure the design and wording of your "Registration" template.

Admin notification
There is no second chance for a first impression - the way you response to your leads and how quickly you do it makes all the difference, that is why we created the admin notification email - it will notify you once a new lead was submitted so you can go and take care of it as soon as you get it.

Merchant intro
After you finished setting up your merchant and it is time for him to start the integration process you will want to send him the login credentials for the Merchant Control Panel (MCP) and the relevant documentation on the Developer Center.
We see no point in repeating the same actions so that is why we created this template - once you click the "Merchant Intro" button at the Data tab under Merchant management a new password will be generated and will be included in the email template to the merchant.

Status change
Following the same logic of repeating actions we added the "Status change" template, once the merchant is ready for LIVE processing you can send out the template notifying the merchant about the status change.
Its a simple template to notify the merchant about the change - feel free to update the template to include additional information on the "Email templates" section under settings.
